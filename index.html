A Virtual Tour of Tabaco City's Arboreal Heritage

Presented by the Geog 192 Field Class (Midyear 2021)
This map showcases the ecological, social, and cultural importance of the various heritage trees found within the city of Tabako. The points on the map correspond to a prominent arboreal feature. Clicking on a point will display a brief summary of the tree's details and the stories surrounding them. This virtual tour intends to display the value and importance of these heritage trees at a more accessible digital level. Additionally, it aims to raise awareness on the risks and vulnerabilities threatening arboreal heritage, such as typhoons, lack of education, and forest denudation. This project was made possible through the efforts of the Geog 192 class of 2021 and the [anong gov't agency ulit hehe]. Photo documentation and geo-tagging of the heritage trees were done by Professor Marco Lagman. Semi-structured interviews were used to acquire descriptions and stories of the trees.
